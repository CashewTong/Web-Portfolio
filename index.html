Robotics

Automated Toy Car

A robotic car built with an Arduino Uno, sonar sensor, servo motor, infrared sensor, motor driver board, and Bluetooth module. The car is powered by two 3.7V batteries and its wheels are powered by four DC motors. With the sonar sensor connected to a servo motor, the car is able to scan a full 180 degree for obstacles ahead to avoid collision. The infrared sensor allows the car to autonomously follow a dark coloured line. As well, the Arduino Uno is connected to a Bluetooth module that allows connectivity to a remote via Bluetooth.

Automation

Automated Claw

This is an aluminum servo motor claw. Using rivets, sheets of thin aluminum, hole punches, copper wire, a servo motor, sensor and an Arduino Uno. It uses a sonar radar to read distances from a flat surface. Within the Arduino Uno board, it was programmed to read distances from the sonar every half a second and as it surpasses 0.5cm it will activate the servo motor to move 180 degrees. Once, the claw recognizes another 0.5cm distance gap from the sonar, it will turn 180 degrees in the opposite direction. The servo motor is connected to the claws with a stiff, copper wire to allow the claw to open and close to grab objects.

Electronics

Alarm Clock

Using 8051 assembly and a AT89LP51RC2 microcontroller, I made an alarm clock that is able to display two different screens. The clock is able to display the time (like in image) and change from AM to PM and vice versa like a regular digital clock. Using an I/O port, a set of buttons are used to switch between clock and alarm mode. During alarm mode, the user is able to set a specific time for an alarm to sound from a small speaker module.

Verilog

Tic-Tac-Toe Game

Using the DE1-SOC and Verilog, a user is able to connect through VGA to a monitor to play a game of Tic-Tac-Toe with a friend. There are 9 switches that correspond to a location on the Tic-Tac-Toe table. Each player take turns flipping a switch to place their symbol. Once a game is tied, the game will restart but if a winner was decided, a line will cross over the winning move.

Cardboard Foldable Chair

Given a scenario of building an affordable, foldable, lightweight chair that is able to be carried for long journeys. Our chair consisted of three major components, a base, seat and supports. The base was built in order to be easily built by allowing pieces to smoothly slide together. The seat is a long piece of cardboard that allows for a comfortable seat but the edge hangs over to give an aesthetic look while supporting the base from twisting. On the back, two support pieces were able to slide in to give the base more support to prevent twisting to occur. After experimenting, twisting is one of the main causes of structural failure in our prototypes. Our chair was able to sustain a consistent weight of 360 pounds while weighing less than 5 pounds.
